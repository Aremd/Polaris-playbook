Common misreads

6 recurring interpretation errors confirmed across testnet sessions J+12→J+28. Each one has caused or could cause a wrong decision.

6 confirmed errors Grounded in real sessions
✗ Wrong "LTF is a liquidation threshold — if it reaches 100%, my position gets liquidated."
✓ Correct LTF is a borrowing capacity ratio. ETH Loan is non-liquidatable. No LTF level triggers forced liquidation.
LTF = ETH Debt / (pETH Collateral × Floor price)

LTF measures how much of your available borrowing capacity you've used. At LTF 100% you simply cannot borrow more — your position stays open indefinitely. The real risk is leverage risk: if ETH drops, your pETH collateral loses market value, but the protocol never forces a close. Monitor floor price trend, not LTF as a danger threshold.

Wrong read → unnecessary panic exit from a healthy ETH Loan position.
✗ Wrong "LTV and LTF are the same metric — both tell me how close I am to liquidation."
✓ Correct LTV and LTF are different metrics for different products with different risk logic. Never conflate them.
MetricProductFormulaLiquidation?
LTV CDP (pUSD borrow) Debt / Collateral value Yes — at MCR breach
LTF ETH Loan ETH Debt / (Collateral × Floor) No — capacity only
Wrong read → applying CDP liquidation logic to ETH Loan, creating false urgency.
✗ Wrong "TCR is dropping — my position is about to get liquidated."
✓ Correct TCR measures system health, not your individual position health. A low TCR increases systemic risk, but your CDP is only at risk if your own LTV breaches the liquidation threshold.

TCR = Total Collateral / Total Debt across all CDPs. It can drop because other users opened high-LTV positions, not because of anything you did. Confirmed J+27: TCR held 215% during ETH −1.2% with no individual liquidations. The danger signal is TCR < 175% combined with your own LTV approaching 65%+ — not TCR alone.

Reflex: always cross-reference TCR + your own LTV. Neither alone is sufficient.
Wrong read → panic exit from a secure position during a system-level stress signal.
✗ Wrong "pETH Gain = 0 on my SP position — the system is quiet, nothing happened."
✓ Correct pETH Gain = 0 means no CDP was liquidated and absorbed by your SP deposit. It does not mean the system is inactive — TCR may have moved, IR may have changed, floor may have shifted.

pETH Gain is a liquidation absorption signal, nothing more. During J+18→J+24 (6-day pause), pETH Gain stayed at 0 the entire time — but IR moved from 4.50% to 4.60% unnoticed. On return, NBR had drifted. Zero pETH Gain is not a green light to skip the full daily reading.

Reflex: pETH Gain > 0 → investigate. pETH Gain = 0 → still read Overview and NBR.
Wrong read → missing silent parameter drift (IR, NBR) during inactivity periods.
✗ Wrong "SP APY is spiking during an ETH drop — something is wrong, I should exit."
✓ Correct SP APY rising during stress is the protocol working as designed. Panic withdrawals reduce SP deposits → APY rises automatically to incentivize redeposits. This is an opportunity, not a danger signal.

Confirmed J+27: ETH −1.2% triggered ~50K pUSD SP withdrawal spike. SP APY auto-adjusted +0.3% within the same session. Users who exited locked in that withdrawal; users who stayed captured the elevated yield. The only reason to exit SP during stress is if pUSD peg drops below $0.95 — not because APY is rising.

Reflex: SP APY ↑ during ETH drop → stay in. Only exit SP on severe depeg (< $0.95).
Wrong read → exiting SP at exactly the moment yield is highest.
✗ Wrong "IR is at 4.6% — that's my borrowing cost."
✓ Correct IR is the protocol gross rate. Your real borrowing cost is Net Borrow Rate = IR minus pETH yield on your collateral. NBR is the only number that determines SP First profitability.
Net Borrow Rate = IR − pETH collateral yield  ·  Net Yield = SP APY − NBR

Confirmed J+25→J+28: IR ranged 4.55–4.60%, but NBR stayed at 1.1–1.3% — less than a third of IR. Using IR as the cost metric would have suggested the strategy was barely profitable; using NBR correctly showed strong Net Yield (~7.6–7.8%). IR softening is in progress by the team — track NBR trend, not IR directly.

Reflex: never quote IR as your cost. Always read Net Borrow Rate on the dashboard.
Wrong read → underestimating SP First profitability, exiting a winning position prematurely.

Metrics circuit

Read left to right = understand the cause. Read right to left = trace back to the source of a problem.

ETH spot → Oracle → LTV (CDP) → TCR system → IR controller → Net Borrow Rate → SP APY → Net Yield
ℹ LTF (ETH Loan) follows its own logic: LTF = ETH Debt / (pETH Collateral × Floor price). Maximum ETH borrow = pETH collateral × floor price. It does not feed into the causal chain above — the ETH Loan is non-liquidatable. LTF indicates remaining borrowing capacity only.
MetricScopeWhat it tells youWhat it does not tell youReflex to develop
LTV CDP Health of your pUSD borrowing position. Distance to liquidation. Does not indicate system health. Low LTV in a low TCR = false sense of security. Always cross-reference LTV + TCR. Never one without the other.
LTF ETH Loan ETH borrowing ratio: ETH Debt / (pETH Collateral × Floor price). Indicates remaining capacity. Does not reflect liquidation risk — ETH Loan is non-liquidatable. Floor price is the health indicator. LTF = leverage gauge, not a danger signal. Monitor floor price trend over 7d.
TCR System Overall system health. Recovery Mode if < 150%. Does not indicate whether your individual position is at risk. TCR < 175% → stay alert even if your LTV is low.
Net Borrow Rate CDP Real cost of debt after pETH yield deduction. The only CDP profitability metric that matters. Not sufficient alone — compare to SP APY to calculate net yield. NBR > SP APY → SP First becomes unprofitable. Act within 24h.
IR controller System Protocol gross rate. IR softening in progress (team). Does not reflect real cost (see Net Borrow Rate). Track the 7d trend, not the instantaneous value.
pUSD peg System Stablecoin health. >$1.01 = demand > supply. <$0.99 = stress. A brief depeg is not a crisis. Automatic repeg observed in 27 min. Depeg > 1h below $0.98 = investigate. Brief = wait.
Floor price pETH System Mechanical protocol growth. Can only go up. Floor ↑ = additional ETH Loan capacity. Does not indicate an ETH Loan liquidation price (none exists). Floor ↑ over 7d → favourable conditions for Leverage Loop.
pETH Gain (SP) CDP Direct signal that a CDP liquidation was absorbed by your SP position. Does not indicate the amount or the TCR impact. pETH Gain > 0 → go to CDP System to understand what happened.
SP APY System SP yield. Rises when depositors panic-exit. A high APY during stress is an opportunity, not a danger. SP APY rising during ETH drop → stay in, do not exit.
POLAR inflation System Dilution pressure. Decreasing = active buyback = strong protocol activity. Does not reflect short-term POLAR price. Inflation < 5% over 7d = accumulation signal.
