Common misreads

6 confirmed interpretation errors from testnet sessions 16→30 March 2026.

✗ Wrong"LTF is a liquidation threshold — if it reaches 100%, my position gets liquidated."
✓ CorrectLTF is a borrowing capacity ratio. ETH Loan is non-liquidatable. No LTF level triggers forced liquidation.
LTF = ETH Debt / (pETH Collateral × Floor price)

At LTF 100% you simply cannot borrow more. Position stays open indefinitely. The real risk is leverage risk, not liquidation.

Wrong read → unnecessary panic exit from a healthy ETH Loan position.
✗ Wrong"LTV and LTF are the same metric — both tell me how close I am to liquidation."
✓ CorrectLTV and LTF are different metrics for different products with different risk logic. Never conflate them.
MetricProductFormulaLiquidation?
LTVCDP (pUSD borrow)Debt / Collateral valueYes — MCR breach
LTFETH LoanETH Debt / (Collat × Floor)No — capacity only
Wrong read → applying CDP liquidation logic to ETH Loan, creating false urgency.
✗ Wrong"TCR is dropping — my position is about to get liquidated."
✓ CorrectTCR measures system health, not your individual position. Your CDP is only at risk if your own LTV breaches threshold.

TCR can drop because other users opened high-LTV positions. Confirmed: TCR held 215% during ETH −1.2% with no liquidations.

Reflex: always cross-reference TCR + your own LTV. Neither alone is sufficient.
Wrong read → panic exit from a secure position during a system-level stress signal.
✗ Wrong"pETH Gain = 0 — nothing happened, system is quiet."
✓ CorrectpETH Gain = 0 means no CDP was liquidated. IR may have changed, NBR may have drifted, floor may have shifted.

During inactivity, NBR rose from 1.42% to 3.60% unnoticed over 11 days (16→27 March). Zero pETH Gain is not a green light to skip the daily reading.

Reflex: pETH Gain = 0 → still read Overview and NBR.
Wrong read → missing silent parameter drift (IR, NBR) during inactivity periods.
✗ Wrong"SP APY is spiking during an ETH drop — something is wrong, I should exit."
✓ CorrectSP APY rising during stress is the protocol working as designed. This is an opportunity, not a danger signal.

ETH −1.2% triggered ~50K pUSD SP withdrawal spike. SP APY auto-adjusted +0.3%. Users who stayed captured elevated yield.

Reflex: SP APY ↑ during ETH drop → stay in. Only exit on severe depeg (< $0.95).
Wrong read → exiting SP at exactly the moment yield is highest.
✗ Wrong"IR is at 5% — that's my borrowing cost."
✓ CorrectIR is the protocol gross rate. Your real cost is Net Borrow Rate = IR minus pETH yield. NBR is the only number that matters.
Net Borrow Rate = IR − pETH collateral yield  ·  Net Yield = SP APY − NBR

IR ranged 4.55–5.08%, but NBR stayed at 1.3–2.9% — well below IR. Using IR as cost metric would have shown false unprofitability.

Reflex: never quote IR as your cost. Always read Net Borrow Rate on the dashboard.
Wrong read → underestimating SP First profitability, exiting a winning position prematurely.
